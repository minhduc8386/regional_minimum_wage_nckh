# Del Carpio Paper Evaluation

## 1. Paper information

- Title: "The Impact of Minimum Wages on Employment, Wages and Welfare: The Case of Vietnam"
- Authors: Ximena Del Carpio, Cuong Nguyen, Ha Nguyen and Choon Wang
- Year: 2013 manuscript; MPRA/RePEc version available as MPRA Paper No. 83677
- Local file: `paper/MPRA_paper_83677.pdf`
- Online source: IDEAS/RePEc and MPRA

## 2. Paper type

The version currently identified in the project is an MPRA/RePEc working paper.

It should therefore be described as a working paper unless a peer-reviewed journal version is found and verified. This matters for the advisor's checklist because a working paper does not automatically carry a journal quartile ranking.

## 3. Journal/Q ranking

No journal/Q ranking is assigned to the MPRA working paper version unless a journal version is found.

Recommended wording:

"Del Carpio et al. is used as a Vietnam context benchmark based on its MPRA working paper version. We do not label it Q1/Q2 because no journal version has been verified."

## 4. Data

The paper uses Vietnam data from:

- Vietnam Enterprise Survey.
- Vietnam Household Living Standards Survey (VHLSS).
- Main period reported in the project notes: 2006, 2008 and 2010.

The study is useful because it combines firm/enterprise-side and household/worker-side perspectives, allowing it to discuss wage employment, self-employment, wages and welfare channels.

## 5. Variables

Main outcomes Y include:

- Wage employment.
- Employment in domestic firms and other firm groups.
- Informal contracts and formal jobs.
- Self-employment.
- Worker wages.
- Household welfare indicators such as poverty, income and expenditure.

Treatment D:

- Minimum wage changes in Vietnam.
- The paper is especially useful for learning how to construct real minimum wage exposure and how to use regional/sectoral variation in minimum wage policy.

Controls W:

- Worker, household, firm and location characteristics depending on the specification.
- Fixed effects are used to absorb unobserved heterogeneity at province, district or firm levels.

## 6. Method

The paper uses traditional econometric methods, including:

- OLS.
- Province fixed effects.
- District fixed effects.
- Firm fixed effects.

The fixed effects structure is highly relevant for the current project because our baseline also starts from OLS/FE/TWFE before moving to DML as a robustness/flexible-control exercise.

## 7. Main results

The paper reports that minimum wage changes in Vietnam:

- Reduce the number of wage workers, especially in domestic firms.
- Reduce wage employment partly through job losses among workers with informal contracts.
- Increase self-employment.
- Raise average wages for workers who keep their jobs.
- Have welfare-related effects, including poverty/income/consumption channels.

These results are important for motivating why a minimum wage policy can affect not only wages but also the allocation of workers across formal jobs, informal contracts and self-employment.

## 8. Limitations

- The currently verified version is a working paper, not a Q-ranked journal article.
- The data are older than the current project's 2018-2024 panel.
- The paper does not use `informal_rate` at province-year level as the main outcome.
- The paper does not use DML or modern causal ML.
- The paper's microdata and replication files are not already available in the current repo.
- The institutional context and data structure differ from the current province-year aggregate panel.

## 9. Role in our project

This paper is used as a Vietnam context benchmark, not as the main Q-ranked paper.

Concrete role:

- Motivate the Vietnam policy context.
- Show that minimum wage changes can affect wage employment, informal contracts and self-employment.
- Provide a useful reference for real minimum wage construction and fixed effects logic.
- Help compare whether the current project's results are directionally consistent with older Vietnam evidence.

The project should not present Del Carpio et al. as the main academic/Q-ranked benchmark unless a journal version is found.
